# Bloom — Because No Mom Should Have to Heal Alone

## Inspiration

I'm the youngest in my family — the only son, born years after my two older sisters. By the time I was growing up, my sisters were already starting families of their own. I watched them give everything to their children — my two nephews, whom I absolutely adore — but I also watched them go through something nobody in our family fully understood.

After their pregnancies, both my sisters quietly struggled. One would cry for no reason she could explain. The other stopped sleeping, stopped eating, stopped being *herself* — and nobody knew what to say. Our family loved them, but love without understanding isn't always enough.

That curiosity stuck with me. I started reading — not as a student, but as a brother who wanted to *get it*. I learned that postpartum depression isn't just "feeling sad." It is a clinically recognized medical condition that affects up to **1 in 5 mothers**, and yet the majority suffer in silence because society has conditioned women to suppress their pain, smile through the exhaustion, and treat motherhood as something that should come naturally.

When this hackathon came up, I knew exactly what I wanted to build. I also know that Google values projects that target **real human problems** — not just technically impressive demos. Research overwhelmingly shows that the single most impactful intervention for postpartum mental health is **having someone to talk to — someone who listens, validates, and doesn't judge.** That one insight became the foundation of Bloom.

## What it does

**Bloom** is a postpartum emotional support platform that gives every new mother a personalized AI companion she can talk to — anytime, about anything — through real-time **voice and video calls**.

But Bloom isn't just another chatbot. Here's what makes it different:

- **Full Personalization** — A mother can name her companion, choose its voice, customize its personality, and give it specific instructions. She shapes *who* she talks to, not the other way around. It's her companion, on her terms.

- **Real-Time Voice & Video Calls** — Using the **Gemini Live API** with native audio streaming, mothers can have natural, conversational phone calls with their companion. It greets them by name. It remembers past conversations. It listens with warmth and responds with care. The companion appears as a **3D animated avatar** (powered by Three.js and the TalkingHead library) with lip-sync and contextual gestures — nodding when she shares something heavy, smiling when she laughs.

- **Therapist Integration** — Because postpartum depression is a *medical condition*, Bloom lets a licensed therapist connect to the mother's account using a secure key. The therapist can:
  - Send clinical instructions directly to the AI companion (shaping *how* it responds).
  - View a **clinical dashboard** with structured conversation analysis — symptom signals, risk flags, concerning excerpts, and recommended actions — all generated by Gemini with structured JSON output.
  - Send direct messages to the mother through the app.

- **Trusted Person System** — Many women don't tell their partners, mothers, or friends what they're really going through. With Bloom, a mother can connect a trusted person via a shareable key. That trusted person gets their own dashboard — written in **plain, non-clinical language** — with a status label, a summary of how she's doing, and gentle, actionable suggestions like *"Bring her favorite tea tonight"* or *"Ask her about the baby's feeding — she mentioned it was stressful."*

- **Intelligent Insights Dashboard** — Every conversation is analyzed by Gemini to extract 8 signal scores (mood, energy, sleep, stress, support, self-kindness, coping, bonding), identify themes, track mood direction, and generate personalized quick tips and YouTube resource recommendations — all without a single clinical form or questionnaire.

- **Bloom Score & Streak** — A gamified engagement score that measures how consistently a mother is *seeking help* — not her mental state. Because the act of reaching out is itself an achievement worth celebrating.

## How we built it

### Architecture

Bloom is a full-stack JavaScript application with a clear separation of concerns:

| Layer | Technology |
|---|---|
| **Frontend** | React 18 + Vite 7 (mobile-first SPA) |
| **Styling** | Tailwind CSS v4 + Radix UI primitives |
| **3D Avatar** | Three.js + TalkingHead library (viseme-based lip-sync, gesture mapping) |
| **Real-Time Voice/Video** | Gemini Live API (`@google/genai`) via custom WebSocket client |
| **Backend** | Express.js v5 (Node.js 20) |
| **AI Agent** | Google Agent Development Kit (`@google/adk`) — `LlmAgent` + `InMemoryRunner` |
| **Analysis Engine** | Gemini 2.5 Pro with structured JSON output (`responseSchema`) |
| **Database** | PostgreSQL on Neon (serverless) |
| **Auth** | Google OAuth 2.0 + JWT |
| **Deployment** | Docker → Google Cloud Run via Cloud Build + Artifact Registry |
| **Secrets** | Google Secret Manager |

### The Companion Pipeline

1. **Onboarding** → The mother names her companion, chooses a voice (e.g., Aoede), optionally writes personality instructions, and selects a 3D avatar.
2. **Call Initiation** → The frontend opens a WebSocket to the Gemini Live API with a rich system instruction that includes the companion name, personality, therapist guidance, and personal memories from past conversations.
3. **Audio Streaming** → A custom `AudioRecorder` captures 16kHz PCM from the mic, streams it as base64 chunks via `sendRealtimeInput()`. Incoming audio is decoded by `AudioStreamer` and played through the Web Audio API.
4. **3D Avatar** → The `TalkingHead` instance renders a Three.js ReadyPlayerMe avatar. A custom **viseme engine** pushes randomized phoneme animations into the avatar's animation queue in sync with AI audio output. A **gesture mapper** parses AI transcript text in real-time and triggers contextual gestures (nods, smiles, empathy expressions).
5. **Barge-In** → Server-side VAD (Voice Activity Detection) with configurable sensitivity handles interruptions. When the user speaks over the AI, all queued audio is flushed, the viseme engine stops, and the model responds only to the latest user input.
6. **Post-Call Analysis** → Transcripts are saved per-turn to PostgreSQL. On call end, Gemini 2.5 Pro analyzes the full transcript with a structured schema producing signal scores, risk assessment, therapist notes, and trusted person guidance — all in one pass.

### The Multi-Role System

Every user connects via a **unique 8-character key** (generated with `nanoid`). The system supports three roles:

- **Mom** — Full access to companion, dashboard, journal, and insights.
- **Therapist** — Connected via key. Sees clinical dashboards with symptom signals, risk flags, and concerning excerpts. Can inject instructions into the AI companion.
- **Trusted Person** — Connected via key. Sees a simplified, warm dashboard with a status label and actionable suggestions — *zero clinical jargon*.

The dashboard insights engine runs parallel Gemini calls for per-call analysis, daily/weekly/monthly rollups, personalized quick tips, and YouTube resource recommendations — with automatic retries, model fallback, and oEmbed validation for video links.

## Challenges we ran into

### 1. Lip-Sync with Streaming Audio
The hardest technical challenge was lip-syncing the 3D avatar with Gemini's streamed audio. The Gemini Live API streams PCM audio in chunks — there's no phoneme timing data, no word-level alignment, nothing. We couldn't use traditional lip-sync approaches that require pre-processed audio.

**Our solution:** We built a **fake viseme engine** that pushes randomized phoneme animations (from a set of 14 visemes: `aa`, `O`, `E`, `I`, `U`, `PP`, `FF`, `TH`, `DD`, `kk`, `CH`, `SS`, [nn](file:///c:/Users/VEER%20Adyani/Desktop/gemini%20hackathon/frontend/src/hooks/useCompanionCall.js#576-580), `RR`) directly into the TalkingHead animation queue. The engine activates when the model turn starts and deactivates (with a 1-second debounce) when the turn completes — not when audio chunks stop arriving, which happens earlier. This distinction was critical; without it, the avatar's mouth would freeze mid-sentence while the AudioStreamer was still playing buffered audio.

### 2. Barge-In Without Echo Cancellation
Client-side barge-in detection was a nightmare. The microphone picks up the AI's own audio playing through the speaker, causing false interruptions — the AI would cut itself off. We tried volume thresholds, but they couldn't distinguish user speech from speaker echo.

**Our solution:** We abandoned client-side barge-in entirely and rely **100% on the server's VAD** (Voice Activity Detection). The Gemini Live API's `interrupted` event handles real interruptions. On our side, we implemented a flag-based ignore system: when an interruption fires, we set `ignoreAudioRef = true` to discard all stale audio chunks until the next `turnComplete` event clears the flag. A subtle but critical edge case: if the model turn had *already completed* but the streamer was still draining old audio, setting the ignore flag would permanently silence all future audio (since no `turnComplete` would ever clear it). We guard against this by only setting the flag when `modelTurnActiveRef` is true.

### 3. Multi-Role Dashboard Insights at Scale
Generating structured insights for three different audiences (mom, therapist, trusted person) from a single conversation transcript required carefully crafted prompts with rigid JSON schemas. Early attempts produced inconsistent output — missing fields, hallucinated scores, or clinically inappropriate language in the trusted person view.

**Our solution:** We use Gemini's `responseSchema` with strict `Type.OBJECT` schemas (60+ required fields across nested objects) combined with a [normalizeCallAnalysis()](file:///c:/Users/VEER%20Adyani/Desktop/gemini%20hackathon/backend/src/services/dashboardInsights.js#81-158) function that clamps scores, deduplicates strings, and sanitizes every field. We also implemented a retry engine with exponential backoff and automatic model fallback for 429/5xx errors.

### 4. Safari Audio Restrictions
Safari blocks `AudioContext` creation and microphone access without a direct user gesture. Our auto-start call flow worked perfectly on Chrome but silently failed on Safari/iOS.

**Our solution:** We detect Safari via `isLikelySafariBrowser()` and show a "Tap to Start Call" button instead of auto-connecting — ensuring the `AudioContext` and `getUserMedia` calls originate from a user gesture event.

## What we learned

- **Postpartum depression is not "baby blues."** It's a condition that deserves the same engineering rigor we give to any health problem. Building Bloom forced us to deeply understand the clinical landscape — the PHQ-9, Edinburgh Postnatal Depression Scale, risk stratification — and then translate all of that into language that a tired, overwhelmed, first-time mom would actually find comforting instead of terrifying.

- **The Gemini Live API is remarkably capable** for building real-time conversational agents, but it requires careful engineering around its streaming model — especially around turn management, barge-in handling, and the gap between "model done generating" and "audio done playing."

- **Google ADK simplifies agent creation** significantly. Our companion agent is defined in ~40 lines of code, with session management, conversation memory, and instruction injection handled by the framework.

- **Structured output from Gemini is a game-changer** for building data-driven dashboards from unstructured conversations. The combination of `responseSchema` + post-processing normalization gives us reliable, typed JSON even from complex emotional analysis prompts.

- **Design matters as much as engineering** when your user is a vulnerable human. Every color choice, every word, every animation in Bloom was chosen to feel warm, safe, and non-clinical — because the last thing a struggling mother needs is an app that feels like a hospital form.

## What's next for Bloom

- **Persistent memory across calls** — Using vector embeddings to give the companion long-term memory of past conversations, so it can naturally reference things the mother shared weeks ago.
- **Mood journaling** — A voice-first journal that captures daily reflections and feeds into the insight engine.
- **Crisis detection pipeline** — Real-time risk scoring during calls with automatic therapist alerts for high-severity signals.
- **Multi-language support** — Extending the companion and lip-sync engine to support Spanish, Hindi, and Arabic.
- **Native mobile app** — Wrapping the PWA in a React Native shell for push notifications and background audio.
